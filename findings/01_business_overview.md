# Business Overview

## What this business does

* The database represents a **multi-category ecommerce business** operating in India.
* The company sells products across categories such as **Apparel, Home & Living, and Consumer Electronics**, offering a broad catalog from multiple brands rather than focusing on a single product line.
* Beyond order processing, the platform supports inventory management, payments, shipping, returns, promotions, loyalty programs, customer segmentation, and marketing attribution. This indicates a mature ecommerce operation with systems in place to manage both customer acquisition and post-purchase experiences.

## Scale

* The business currently has **10,000 registered customers**, providing a solid customer base for analysis.
* Customers have placed **40,000 orders**, averaging **4 orders per purchasing customer**, which suggests repeat purchasing rather than one-time transactions.
* The product catalog contains approximately **4,000 products** across **18 categories** from **120 brands**, reflecting a diverse marketplace.
* During the available data period, the business generated **₹298.75 million (₹29.88 crore)** in revenue with an **Average Order Value (AOV) of ₹33,949.33**, indicating that customers typically purchase relatively high-value items.
* The transaction data spans **16 March 2026 to 14 June 2026** (approximately three months). This provides a useful snapshot of current operations, although it is not sufficient for assessing long-term trends or seasonality.

## What I noticed

* Customers appear to make repeat purchases, with an average of **4 orders per purchasing customer**, indicating encouraging customer retention.
* The database captures detailed marketing information such as acquisition channels, UTM parameters, and customer lifecycle stages, suggesting the business places significant emphasis on measuring marketing performance and customer engagement.
* A data quality check found **no orphan customer IDs**—every order references a valid customer record. This indicates good referential integrity between the core business tables and increases confidence in the reliability of future analyses.
