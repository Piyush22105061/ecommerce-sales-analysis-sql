# Business Health Assessment

## Executive Summary

The business generated **gross revenue in the low millions of dollars** over the reporting period, indicating a business operating at a meaningful commercial scale rather than an early-stage operation. Revenue calculations were based on validated order subtotals, where each order's subtotal exactly matched the sum of its underlying line items (`quantity × unit price`). Cancelled orders were excluded from gross revenue calculations, while net revenue was calculated after accounting for successful refunds and taxes on shipped and delivered orders.

## Revenue Trajectory

Monthly revenue analysis shows that the business is **not experiencing steady linear growth**. Revenue increased significantly from March to April before declining during May. Although June also shows a substantial decline, it should not be interpreted as deteriorating business performance because the available data only covers part of the month, making comparisons misleading.

The April-to-May decline is the first meaningful trend observed between two complete months and suggests a reduction in sales activity after a strong April. This could be driven by seasonality, the conclusion of a promotional campaign, or lower customer demand. While the overall business remains healthy from a revenue perspective, the decline warrants further investigation.

Net revenue closely follows the same pattern as gross revenue, indicating that refunds and taxes reduce realized revenue but do not materially change the underlying sales trend.

## Average Order Value (AOV)

Average Order Value remained within the expected range for an ecommerce business and was calculated by dividing gross revenue by the number of qualifying orders. No abnormal spikes or unusually low values were observed, suggesting that the business maintained relatively consistent customer purchasing behaviour throughout the reporting period.

Since AOV remained comparatively stable while revenue fluctuated between months, the revenue changes are more likely attributable to changes in **order volume** rather than customers purchasing significantly larger or smaller baskets.

## Key Observation

The most significant business event during the reporting period is the decline in revenue from April to May. Because both months represent complete reporting periods, this change reflects a genuine shift in business performance rather than incomplete data. Management should investigate whether this decline resulted from reduced marketing activity, lower customer acquisition, weaker repeat purchases, or seasonal demand patterns.

Overall, the business demonstrates healthy revenue generation with stable customer spending per order. Future growth is therefore likely to depend more on increasing completed order volume than on increasing basket value alone.
